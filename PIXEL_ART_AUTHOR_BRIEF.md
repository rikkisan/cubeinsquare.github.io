# Pixel Art Author Brief

This note is here to make future writing easy. The tool pages for `skin-editor` and `texture-painter` already have strong structure. Your own writing can now sit on top of that structure as the personal, lived layer: the part that sounds like a real artist or teammate, not just a polished site.

The goal is not to explain the entire tool again. The goal is to add the part only you can give:

- what you actually notice when drawing
- what tends to go wrong in practice
- what makes a skin or texture feel alive instead of merely finished
- what kind of shortcuts are fake shortcuts

Keep the tone practical and personal. It can be warm, opinionated, and a little stubborn. It does **not** need to sound neutral or encyclopedic.

## Best insertion points

### 1. `skin-editor/index.html`

The best places for your own writing are:

1. After `Why painting on the 3D player model matters`
2. Inside `Pixel-art habits that make skins read better`
3. Inside `A practical review checklist before export`

That means your text should focus on:

- how you think about outfit readability
- how much detail is too much on a Minecraft body
- what makes a skin feel expressive instead of noisy
- how you judge sleeves, hats, faces, and repeated accents

### 2. `texture-painter/index.html`

The best places for your own writing are:

1. After `How to think about item icons versus block faces`
2. Inside `Pixel-art techniques worth practicing here`
3. Inside `A review pass before you send the PNG into a pack`

That means your text should focus on:

- how you decide whether an icon is readable
- when a texture is too detailed
- how you think about clusters, contrast, material, and light
- what makes a texture "Minecraft enough" versus too muddy or too overworked

## Recommended shape for your writing

The easiest format is:

1. one short intro paragraph
2. three or four compact principles
3. one "how I check myself" paragraph

That usually lands around `180-400` words per block, which is enough to feel authored without turning the page into an essay.

## Writing prompts for `skin-editor`

You do **not** need to answer all of these. Pick the ones that feel real.

- What makes a skin feel instantly readable to you?
- When does a design become too busy?
- How do you think about the face compared with the clothes?
- What usually carries the character fantasy first: silhouette, palette, or small details?
- When do asymmetrical details help, and when do they just make the skin messy?
- What do you check first after rotating the model?
- What is the most common mistake people make when drawing an RP outfit?

### Example angle

> A skin can be technically clean and still feel dead if the silhouette says nothing. For me the first question is always whether the outfit reads before the details do.

That is the kind of sentence that helps. It is not generic tutorial text; it sounds like judgment born from doing the work.

## Writing prompts for `texture-painter`

- How do you know an item icon is actually readable?
- When do extra pixels stop helping?
- What usually matters more for you: contrast, silhouette, or material texture?
- How do you treat block edges differently from item edges?
- What makes a block texture tile well?
- What makes a texture feel too polished for vanilla Minecraft?
- How do you decide whether `16x16` is enough?

### Example angle

> If I still need to explain what the icon is after zooming out, the problem is not "lack of polish." The problem is that the shape was never clear.

That is exactly the kind of sentence worth keeping.

## Two good tones to aim for

### Tone A: calm practical artist

- "I usually start by..."
- "What tends to fail first is..."
- "If the texture only works at huge zoom, it still is not solved."

### Tone B: opinionated builder

- "A lot of people add detail before they earn it."
- "Minecraft forgives bold shapes faster than muddy shading."
- "If everything sparkles, nothing becomes the focal point."

Both are good. The site can carry a real voice.

## What to avoid

- giant abstract paragraphs about "art"
- fake-neutral textbook language
- repeating tool features instead of talking about drawing decisions
- saying what the user "must" do when the real answer is taste plus testing

## Easiest workflow

When you are ready, just write one rough block under one of these headers:

- `Skin editor note`
- `Texture painter note`

It can be messy. It can be half-finished. It can be written like a message, not like an article.

Then we can turn it into final copy and place it exactly where it belongs.
